What's in your trash?

Many people do not realize how much plastic and waste they accumulate throughout the years. That plastic water bottle you drink everyday turns into 365 bottles a year scattered on the Earth. Everyday an American consumer creates under five pounds of trash each day. While that doesn't sound like much all of the waste adds up to 1,642 pounds a year. From those plastic straws you trash to plastic shampoo bottles you use, most of these items end up in the environment especially our oceans.

3. Minimize Waste & Plastic Usage

There are many sustainable products out on the market. Swapping out disposable products for reusable products made out of sustainable materials can heavily lessen the amount of waste you make yearly. There are also more products being made out of decomposable materials that decomposes much faster than products made out of plastic. Here are some product swap-outs!

Cloth bags are a great alternative for plastic bags. Bring your cloth bags to the grocery store instead of using the plastic bags
No more plastic straws! Use metal reusable straws that are easy to wash and they even sell collapsable ones for easy storage
A great swap for plastic toothbrushes are bamboo toothbrushes. By using bamboo toothbrushes you are eliminating one of the forms of ocean plastic pollution
Shampoo and conditioning plastic bottles greatly contribute to plastic waste/contamination. An alternative is shampoo and conditioning bars that are not only environmentally friendly, but also have less toxic ingredients
